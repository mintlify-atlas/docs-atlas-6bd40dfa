Food For All DC provides comprehensive reporting capabilities to track service delivery, client demographics, and organizational impact. The system includes three main report types: Summary Reports, Client Reports, and Referral Agency Reports.

## Accessing Reports

Navigate to the **Reports** section from the main navigation menu. From here you can access:

- **Summary Report** - Aggregate statistics and metrics
- **Client Report** - Detailed client-level data
- **Referral Agencies Report** - Referral source tracking and metrics

## Summary Report

The Summary Report provides high-level statistics about your service delivery over a specified time period.

### Generating a Summary Report

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Summary Report">
    Click **Reports** > **Summary Report** from the navigation menu.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select Date Range">
    Choose the reporting period using the date range picker:
    - Start date
    - End date
    
    Common periods: Last month, Last quarter, Year-to-date, Custom range
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Click Generate Report">
    Click the **Generate Report** button to compile the data.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Review Results">
    The report displays in organized sections with key metrics and statistics.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Summary Report Sections

#### Basic Output

Core service delivery metrics:
- **Households Served (Duplicated)** - Total number of deliveries made (counts repeat deliveries)
- **Households Served (Unduplicated)** - Unique households served during the period
- **People Served (Duplicated)** - Total individuals across all deliveries
- **People Served (Unduplicated)** - Unique individuals served
- **Bags Delivered** - Total food bags distributed
- **New Households** - First-time clients during the period
- **New People** - New individuals added
- **Active Clients** - Clients who received at least one delivery
- **Lapsed Clients** - Previously active clients with no recent deliveries

#### Demographics

Population breakdown:
- **New Seniors** - Newly enrolled seniors (65+)
- **Total Seniors** - All seniors served
- **New Single Parents** - Newly enrolled single-parent households
- **New Adults** - Adult household members added
- **Total Adults** - All adults served
- **New Children** - Children added to served households
- **Total Children** - All children in served households

#### Health Conditions

Health-related statistics:
- **Physical Ailments** - Count of clients with conditions like diabetes, hypertension, heart disease, kidney disease, cancer
- **Physical Disability** - Clients with reported physical disabilities
- **Mental Health Conditions** - Clients with reported mental health needs

#### Referrals

Referral source tracking:
- **New Client Referrals** - Clients referred during the period
- **New Referral Agency Names** - New referral organizations added

#### Dietary Restrictions

Breakdown of dietary needs:
- Halal, Vegan, Vegetarian
- Kidney Friendly, Heart Friendly
- Low Sodium, Low Sugar
- Soft Food, Microwave Only, No Cooking Equipment
- Food Allergens
- Other restrictions
- No restrictions

#### Food as Medicine (FAM)

- **Clients Receiving Medically Tailored Food** - Clients enrolled in FAM program with specific health-related dietary needs

#### Tags

Breakdown by client tags (if used):
- Count of clients with each tag during the reporting period

### Exporting Summary Reports

<Steps>
  <Step title="Generate Report">
    First generate the report for your desired date range.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Click Export">
    Click the **Export to CSV** button at the bottom of the report.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Download File">
    The report downloads as a CSV file containing all metrics and their values.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  Summary report exports include the date range in the filename for easy organization.
</Info>

## Client Report

The Client Report provides detailed information about individual clients and their service history.

### Generating a Client Report

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Client Report">
    Click **Reports** > **Client Report** from the navigation menu.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Set Criteria">
    Configure the report parameters:
    - Date range for delivery history
    - Filters (active status, delivery frequency, tags, etc.)
    - Sort order
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Generate Report">
    Click **Generate Report** to compile the client data.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Review Results">
    The report displays a detailed table with one row per client.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Client Report Data Fields

The Client Report typically includes:

**Personal Information:**
- Name (first and last)
- Date of birth
- Gender
- Ethnicity
- Language

**Contact Information:**
- Phone number(s)
- Email address
- Address (street, city, state, zip)
- Ward and quadrant

**Household Information:**
- Number of adults
- Number of children
- Number of seniors
- Total household size
- Head of household type

**Service Details:**
- Start date
- End date (if applicable)
- Delivery frequency
- Last delivery date
- Total deliveries received (in date range)
- Active status

**Special Needs:**
- Dietary restrictions
- Food allergens
- Delivery instructions
- Physical ailments
- Disabilities
- Mental health conditions

**Administrative:**
- Referral entity
- TEFAP certification
- Tags
- Notes

### Filtering Client Reports

You can filter the Client Report by:

- **Active Status** - Active or inactive clients only
- **Date Range** - Clients served during a specific period
- **Delivery Frequency** - Weekly, 2x-monthly, monthly
- **Tags** - Clients with specific tags
- **Ward** - Geographic area
- **Dietary Restrictions** - Specific dietary needs
- **Referral Source** - Specific referring organization

### Exporting Client Reports

Client Reports can be exported to CSV with all fields included. This is useful for:

- Sharing data with partner organizations
- Creating custom analysis in spreadsheet software
- Generating mail merge documents
- Compliance and audit requirements

## Referral Agencies Report

The Referral Agencies Report tracks which organizations are referring clients to your service.

### Generating a Referral Agencies Report

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Report">
    Click **Reports** > **Referral Agencies Report** from the navigation.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select Date Range">
    Choose the time period to analyze referral sources.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Generate Report">
    Click **Generate Report** to compile referral data.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Review Results">
    The report shows referral agencies and their metrics.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Referral Agency Metrics

For each referral source, the report shows:

- **Agency/Organization Name**
- **Total Clients Referred** - All-time referrals
- **New Clients in Period** - Referrals during the selected date range
- **Active Clients** - Currently receiving services
- **Inactive Clients** - No longer receiving services
- **Total Deliveries** - Deliveries made to referred clients
- **Referral Dates** - When clients were first referred

### Using Referral Reports

Referral Agency Reports help you:

- **Identify top referral sources** - Focus partnership efforts on productive relationships
- **Track referral trends** - See which sources are growing or declining
- **Measure partnership impact** - Demonstrate value to referring organizations
- **Plan outreach** - Identify underutilized referral sources
- **Report to funders** - Show community partnerships and reach

## Report Interpretation Tips

<Accordion title="What's the difference between duplicated and unduplicated counts?">
  **Duplicated counts** include repeat deliveries - if one household receives 4 deliveries in a month, they're counted 4 times.
  
  **Unduplicated counts** count each unique household only once, regardless of how many deliveries they received.
  
  Use duplicated counts to measure total service volume. Use unduplicated counts to measure reach and unique clients served.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How do I track program growth?">
  Compare these metrics across time periods:
  - New Households per month
  - Total Active Clients (trending up = growth)
  - Unduplicated Households Served
  
  Look for steady increases in new client intake and decreasing lapsed client rates.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How do I use FAM metrics?">
  The Food as Medicine (FAM) metrics track clients receiving medically tailored food based on specific health conditions. Use these to:
  - Report on health-focused programming
  - Track clients with chronic conditions
  - Demonstrate impact on health outcomes
  - Support grant applications for health-related funding
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What if my numbers seem wrong?">
  First verify:
  1. The date range is correct
  2. Client data is up-to-date (check for missing delivery records)
  3. Filters aren't excluding expected clients
  
  If issues persist, check:
  - Data entry accuracy (duplicate clients, incorrect dates)
  - System synchronization (recent updates may not be reflected)
  - Deleted records (deleted clients/deliveries won't appear in reports)
</Accordion>

## Troubleshooting

<Accordion title="Report generation is slow">
  Large date ranges or datasets can take time to process. To speed up reports:
  - Use shorter date ranges
  - Apply filters to reduce data volume
  - Generate reports during off-peak hours
  - Export and use spreadsheet tools for complex analysis
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Missing data in reports">
  If expected data is missing:
  1. Verify the date range includes the period when deliveries occurred
  2. Check that clients have complete profiles (missing fields won't be counted)
  3. Ensure deliveries are properly recorded in the calendar
  4. Confirm client active status (inactive clients may be filtered out)
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Export file won't open">
  CSV exports should open in any spreadsheet program (Excel, Google Sheets, etc.). If you have trouble:
  - Try a different program
  - Check the file wasn't corrupted during download
  - Ensure you have permission to download files in your browser
  - Try exporting again
</Accordion>
