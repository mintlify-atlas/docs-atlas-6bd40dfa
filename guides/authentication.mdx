## Overview

The Food For All DC platform uses secure authentication to protect client data and ensure only authorized staff can access the system. This guide covers logging in, password management, and security best practices.

## Logging In

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to the login page">
    Open your web browser and go to the Food For All DC application URL provided by your administrator.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enter your credentials">
    - Enter your registered email address in the **Email** field
    - Enter your password in the **Password** field
    - Press Enter or click the **Login** button
  </Step>

  <Step title="Access the system">
    Upon successful login, you'll be automatically redirected to the Clients page where you can begin working.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
The system will remember your session. If you're already logged in, visiting the login page will automatically redirect you to the application.
</Note>

## Password Requirements

To maintain security, all passwords must meet these requirements:

- **Minimum length:** 8 characters
- Must be unique and not previously used
- Consider using a mix of uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and special characters for stronger security

<Warning>
Never share your password with anyone. Each staff member should have their own individual account.
</Warning>

## Resetting Your Password

If you've forgotten your password, follow these steps:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click Forgot Password">
    On the login page, click the **Forgot password?** link below the password field.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enter your email address">
    In the dialog that appears, enter the email address associated with your account.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Submit the request">
    Click **Send Email** to receive a password reset link.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Check your email">
    Look for an email from Food For All DC with the subject line about password reset. If you have an account, you should receive it within a few minutes.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create a new password">
    Click the link in the email and follow the instructions to create a new password that meets the requirements.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
Check your spam folder if you don't see the password reset email within 5 minutes.
</Tip>

## Login Troubleshooting

### Common Error Messages

**"Invalid email or password"**
- Double-check that you've entered the correct email address
- Ensure your password is typed correctly (passwords are case-sensitive)
- Try resetting your password if you're unsure

**"Please enter both email and password"**
- Make sure both fields are filled in before clicking Login

**"An error occurred during login"**
- Check your internet connection
- Try refreshing the page and logging in again
- Contact your administrator if the problem persists

## Security Best Practices

<Steps>
  <Step title="Use a strong, unique password">
    Create a password that's difficult to guess and don't reuse passwords from other accounts.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Log out when finished">
    Always log out when you're done using the system, especially on shared computers.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Keep credentials private">
    Never write down your password or share it via email, text, or messaging apps.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Report suspicious activity">
    If you notice any unusual activity in your account, report it to your administrator immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## User Roles

Your account is assigned one of the following roles, which determines what features you can access:

- **Admin** - Full access to all features including user management
- **Manager** - Access to client management, scheduling, and reporting
- **Client Intake** - Access to client profiles and intake processes
- **Driver** - Access to delivery routes and schedules

<Note>
Your role is assigned by an administrator. If you need access to additional features, contact your system administrator.
</Note>

## Getting Help

If you continue to experience login issues:

1. Contact your system administrator
2. Provide details about the error message you're seeing
3. Include the time you attempted to log in

Your administrator can verify your account status and help resolve access issues.
