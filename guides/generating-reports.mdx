## Overview

The reporting system provides insights into service delivery, client demographics, and program impact. This guide covers how to generate different types of reports and export data.

## Available Report Types

Food For All DC offers several report types:

- **Summary Report** - Comprehensive program statistics and demographics
- **Client Report** - Individual client service details
- **Referral Agencies Report** - Referral source tracking and analysis
- **Caseworker Report** - Caseworker performance and client load

## Accessing Reports

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Reports">
    Click **Reports** in the main navigation menu.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select report type">
    Choose the type of report you want to generate from the tabs or dropdown menu.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Generating a Summary Report

The Summary Report provides comprehensive program statistics.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select date range">
    - Click the **Start Date** field and choose the beginning date
    - Click the **End Date** field and choose the ending date
    - The date range determines which data is included in the report
  </Step>

  <Step title="Click Generate Report">
    Click the **Generate Report** button to create the report.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Wait for processing">
    A loading indicator will appear while the system:
    - Retrieves client data
    - Processes delivery records
    - Calculates statistics
    - Compiles demographics
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review the results">
    The report displays in organized sections with key metrics and totals.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
Report generation time depends on the date range and amount of data. Larger date ranges may take longer to process.
</Note>

## Understanding Summary Report Sections

### Basic Output

Core program metrics:

- **Households Served (Duplicated)** - Total household delivery count including repeats
- **Households Served (Unduplicated)** - Unique households served
- **People Served (Duplicated)** - Total individual delivery count including repeats
- **People Served (Unduplicated)** - Unique individuals served
- **Bags Delivered** - Total number of food bags delivered
- **New Households** - Households receiving their first delivery in the date range
- **New People** - Individuals receiving their first delivery in the date range
- **Active Clients** - Clients with deliveries during the period
- **Lapsed Clients** - Previously active clients with no recent deliveries

### Demographics

Population breakdown:

- **New Seniors** - Seniors (65+) in new households
- **Total Seniors** - All seniors served
- **New Single Parents** - New single-parent households
- **New Adults** - Adults (18-64) in new households
- **Total Adults** - All adults served
- **New Children** - Children in new households
- **Total Children** - All children served

### Health Conditions

Clients reporting:

- **Physical Ailments** - Diabetes, hypertension, heart disease, kidney disease, cancer
- **Physical Disabilities** - Mobility or physical limitations
- **Mental Health Conditions** - Mental health diagnoses

### Referrals

Referral tracking:

- **New Client Referrals** - Clients referred during the period
- **New Referral Agency Names** - New referring organizations

### Dietary Restrictions

Clients requiring:

- Lactose Intolerant options
- Microwave Only meals
- Diabetes Friendly foods
- No Cans
- Food Allergen accommodations
- No Cooking Equipment options
- Gluten Free
- Soft Food
- Halal
- Vegan
- Low Sodium
- Low Sugar
- Heart Friendly
- Vegetarian
- Kidney Friendly
- Other restrictions
- No Restrictions

### Food as Medicine (FAM)

- **Clients Receiving Medically Tailored Food** - Clients in FAM program

### Tags

Custom categorizations tracked by your organization.

<Tip>
Use consistent date ranges (e.g., monthly, quarterly) for easier period-over-period comparisons.
</Tip>

## Exporting Reports

<Steps>
  <Step title="Generate the report">
    First create the report following the steps above.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Click Export">
    Click the **Export to CSV** button at the top of the report.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save the file">
    The report downloads as a CSV file that can be:
    - Opened in Excel or Google Sheets
    - Imported into other analysis tools
    - Shared with stakeholders
    - Archived for record-keeping
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
CSV exports include all report sections and preserve the organizational structure for easy analysis.
</Note>

## Client Report

Generate detailed client-level data:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select Client Report">
    Click the **Client Report** tab.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Choose parameters">
    Select:
    - Date range for delivery data
    - Specific clients (or all clients)
    - Fields to include in the report
  </Step>

  <Step title="Generate and export">
    Click **Generate** to create the report, then export to CSV for detailed analysis.
  </Step>
</Steps>

The Client Report includes:

- Client names and contact information
- Household composition
- Delivery dates and frequency
- Referral source
- Dietary restrictions
- Service start and end dates
- Tags and categorizations

## Report Date Range Selection

### Common Date Ranges

**Monthly Reports:**
- Start: First day of the month
- End: Last day of the month
- Example: 1/1/2024 to 1/31/2024

**Quarterly Reports:**
- Q1: January 1 - March 31
- Q2: April 1 - June 30
- Q3: July 1 - September 30
- Q4: October 1 - December 31

**Annual Reports:**
- Start: January 1
- End: December 31
- Example: 1/1/2024 to 12/31/2024

**Custom Periods:**
- Grant periods
- Program cycles
- Fiscal years

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select start date">
    Click the start date field and choose from the calendar.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select end date">
    Click the end date field and choose from the calendar.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Verify date range">
    Double-check that the dates match your reporting period before generating.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
Date ranges are inclusive - both the start and end dates are included in the report calculations.
</Warning>

## Understanding Report Calculations

### Duplicated vs. Unduplicated Counts

**Duplicated counts** include every instance:
- If a household receives 4 deliveries, they count 4 times
- Useful for tracking total service volume

**Unduplicated counts** count each household/person once:
- Same household with 4 deliveries counts as 1
- Useful for tracking unique individuals served

### New vs. Total Metrics

**New** metrics count only:
- Clients who started service during the date range
- First-time referrals within the period

**Total** metrics count all:
- All clients served, regardless of start date
- All referrals, including historical

### Active vs. Lapsed Clients

**Active clients:**
- Have at least one delivery in the reporting period
- Currently receiving services

**Lapsed clients:**
- Previously had deliveries
- No deliveries in the reporting period
- May have reached end date or discontinued service

<Note>
Understanding these calculation methods helps you interpret report results accurately and answer stakeholder questions.
</Note>

## Report Best Practices

<Steps>
  <Step title="Generate reports regularly">
    Create reports on a consistent schedule:
    - Monthly for operational tracking
    - Quarterly for board meetings
    - Annually for grants and funders
  </Step>

  <Step title="Archive reports">
    Save and organize exported reports:
    - Use clear file naming (e.g., "Summary_Report_Q1_2024.csv")
    - Store in a consistent location
    - Keep historical reports for trend analysis
  </Step>

  <Step title="Verify data quality">
    Before finalizing reports:
    - Check for missing client information
    - Verify date ranges are correct
    - Review calculations for accuracy
  </Step>

  <Step title="Compare periods">
    Generate reports for comparable time frames to track:
    - Growth trends
    - Seasonal patterns
    - Program impact over time
  </Step>

  <Step title="Share strategically">
    Tailor reports to your audience:
    - Executive summaries for leadership
    - Detailed data for program staff
    - Key metrics for funders
    - Impact stories for donors
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Troubleshooting

**Report shows zero results**
- Verify that the date range includes deliveries
- Check that clients have complete profiles
- Ensure data was properly saved during the period

**Numbers seem incorrect**
- Review the date range selection
- Understand duplicated vs. unduplicated counts
- Check that all deliveries were properly recorded

**Export file won't open**
- Ensure you have appropriate software (Excel, Google Sheets)
- Try opening with a different application
- Check that the download completed successfully

**Report is taking too long to generate**
- Try a smaller date range
- Generate during off-peak hours
- Contact your administrator if delays persist

<Warning>
Always verify report data before sharing externally. Double-check calculations and date ranges to ensure accuracy.
</Warning>
